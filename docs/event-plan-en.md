# EVERSEE Creative Forum — Berlin, June 2026

## About the Event

A B2B brainstorm at the intersection of two worlds: technology companies building equipment and solutions for live entertainment, and creative professionals — show directors, circus creators, ceremony designers, artistic and technical directors.

**Goal** — for both sides to understand each other's needs and capabilities, discover collaboration opportunities, and co-create ideas that neither side would come up with alone.

**Format**: 3 days (Thu–Sat), Berlin, 50–100 participants.

This is the first event in a planned series. EVERSEE aims to establish tech × creative workshops and hackathons as its core business.

## Structure

**Day 1 (Thu) — DISCOVER**: each side presents its world. "Here's what we can do / here's what we need." Evening: group dinner and Alizée show.

**Day 2 (Fri) — CREATE**: mixed teams tackle real-world challenges together. Evening: dinner and show at Friedrichstadt-Palast.

**Day 3 (Sat) — VISION**: morning strategy session on future formats (workshops, hackathons), followed by a backstage tour at Friedrichstadt-Palast.

### Speed Matching — In Detail

This is one of the program's key elements and a powerful selling point for ticket sales. In essence, every participant gets a **free market research session** — a series of short, focused live conversations with professionals from the other side of the industry.

**For tech companies**: in one hour, you'll speak with 8–10 show directors, artistic directors, and producers. You'll hear firsthand what they need, what frustrates them, and what they wish existed. These are the kind of insights that marketing departments pay research agencies tens of thousands for.

**For creatives**: in one hour, you'll see 8–10 technologies and solutions you didn't know existed. Every conversation is a potential tool for your next project.

#### How It Works

**Who moves, who stays**: tech representatives sit at numbered tables (one per table). Creatives rotate from table to table on a signal. This way, tech companies don't waste time moving and can keep demo materials laid out.

**5-minute conversation format**:
- 0:00–1:00 — tech introduces themselves: "We build X, here's what it can do" (rehearsed in advance)
- 1:00–2:00 — creative introduces themselves: "I do Y, here's what I need"
- 2:00–5:00 — open conversation: where's the overlap? what would be interesting to try together?

**Number of rounds**: 10 rounds × 5 min + 1 min transition = 60 min. With 12 tech tables and 30+ creatives, each creative talks to roughly a third of the tech companies; each tech company meets roughly every third creative.

**Participant cards**: everyone receives a pre-printed card — name, company/project, 2–3 lines on "what I do" and "what I'm looking for." On the back — a QR code with contact details. After each conversation, participants exchange cards or tick "want to follow up."

**Follow-up list**: after the session, organizers collect the marked cards and send each participant their match list the next morning. This fuels B2B meetings on Day 2 and post-forum contacts.

#### Why This Sells Tickets

On the website and in outreach, position it like this:

> **10 live conversations with industry leaders in 1 hour.** No booths, no brochures, no business cards piling up. You sit across from a decision-maker and have their undivided attention for 5 minutes. For tech — it's a focus group with Europe's top creatives. For creatives — a personal technology showroom.

## Key Considerations

**Facilitation** — this is not a lecture-based conference, it's a brainstorm. A strong host is essential — someone who manages the room's energy and prevents anyone from slipping into monologues.

**Audience balance** — the ideal ratio is roughly 40% tech / 60% creative (or 50/50). Too much tech turns it into a trade show. Too many creatives turns it into a complaint session.

**Language** — given the participant mix (Italian, Swiss, Dutch, Canadian, German), the working language is English. This should be stated explicitly.

**Venue** — a brainstorm format requires a space with one main hall + 5–6 breakout rooms for group work. Not a theatre — more of a loft or coworking space. Ideally in central Berlin, within reasonable distance of both Friedrichstadt-Palast and the Alizée venue.

**Evening shows as part of the program** — tickets to both shows should be included in the participation fee (or in specific ticket tiers). These aren't optional add-ons — they're integral to the experience.

**Restaurants** — book in advance for the full group (50–100 people). Two restaurants needed: one near the Alizée venue (Thu), one near Friedrichstadt-Palast (Fri). Format: long communal tables for conversation, not 4-person seating.
