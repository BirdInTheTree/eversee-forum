# EVERSEE CREATIVE SUMMIT

## BERLIN, JUNE 2026

---

## About the Event

A high-level B2B brainstorm at the intersection of two worlds: technology companies that build equipment and solutions for live entertainment, and creative professionals — show directors, circus creators, concert designers, ceremony producers, artistic and technical directors, choreographers, performers, creative producers, props masters, immersive designers, scenic painters, and production designers and many others.

**The goal**: for both sides to understand each other's needs and capabilities, find real opportunities for collaboration, and co-create what neither side would come up with alone.

This is the first event in a series. EVERSEE is building a platform of tech × creative formats — workshops and creative hackathons — for the live entertainment industry.

**Format**: 3 days, Berlin, 50–100 curated participants.

---

## Three-Day Structure

**Day 1 — DISCOVER**: each side presents its world. "Here's what we can do. Here's what we need." Evening — group dinner and show (Alizée — Cirque du Soleil or BLINDED by DELIGHT — Friedrichstadt-Palast).

**Day 2 — CREATE**: mixed teams tackle real challenges together. Evening — dinner, show (Alizée — Cirque du Soleil or BLINDED by DELIGHT — Friedrichstadt-Palast), and afterparty.

**Day 3 — VISION**: morning strategy session on EVERSEE formats, then a backstage tour of Friedrichstadt-Palast.

## Speed Matching — How It Works

Tech company representatives sit at numbered tables (one per table). Creatives rotate from table to table on a signal. This way, tech companies stay in place with their demo materials ready, while creatives move through the room.

**5-minute conversation format:**

- 0:00–1:00 — Tech: "Here's what our technology does and what it makes possible"
- 1:00–2:00 — Creative: "Here's how I work, what I'm trying to achieve, and where I hit limits"
- 2:00–5:00 — Open conversation: what does the other side struggle with that you had no idea about? What seems obvious to you
   but is a revelation to them?

**Participant cards**: everyone receives a pre-printed card — name, company/project, 2–3 lines on "what I do" and "what I'm looking for." QR code with contact details on the back. After each conversation, participants exchange cards.

~10 rounds × 5 min + 1 min transition = 60 minutes. 

---

**10 live conversations with industry leaders in one hour.** No booths, no brochures, no business cards piling up. You sit across from a decision-maker and have their full attention for 5 minutes.

**For tech companies** — a live focus group with Europe's top creatives. In one hour, you'll talk to 8–10 show directors, artistic directors, and producers. You'll hear firsthand what they need, what frustrates them, and what tools they wish existed.

**For creatives** — a personal technology showroom. In one hour, you'll discover 8–10 technologies and solutions you knew little about. Every conversation is a potential tool for your next project.
